Hi, I'm Annalisa Baronetto.

As I near the conclusion of my PhD journey, I am actively exploring roles within research and development, aiming to apply my skills and knowledge in contributing to groundbreaking advancements.

About Me

I am a dedicated researcher specializing in wearable devices such as smart garments, and harnessing the power of artificial intelligence algorithms for context activity recognition. My expertise lies in creating advanced algorithms that decipher human activities from data collected by these devices. With a primary focus on healthcare, I apply these cutting-edge solutions to monitor physiological parameters and enhance patient well-being. Through this work, I am contributing to the evolution of healthcare by seamlessly integrating technology to provide invaluable insights and improve medical outcomes.

Background

I hold an M.Sc. degree in Biomedical Engineering, received in 2017 by the Politecnico di Torino, Italy. Following my education, I gained practical experience during an internship at Philips Spa, Italy. My role involved overseeing post-market surveillance for medical devices in the IIG market (Italy, Israel, and Greece), and I collaborated effectively with remote teams from these countries, showcasing my dedication to cross-border coordination.

In 2019, I embarked on a PhD journey at the Chair of Digital Health at FAU Erlangen-Nuremberg University, Germany. This marked a turning point in my career as I delved into innovative projects centered around crafting wearable devices specifically designed for telemedicine applications. My primary focus was on innovative solutions involving smart garments, soft electronics, and additive manufacturing technology. During this phase, I gained expertise in artificial intelligence while designing AI-based algorithms to facilitate personalized wearable prototypes, tailored to individual body sizes. I also led the development and implementation of state-of-the-art methods, including deep learning models like EfficientNet and Audio Transformers, to enable the detection and classification of body sounds for health monitoring. I personally supervised clinical studies to evaluate the comfort and wearability on patients of designed prototypes, providing invaluable insights for refining my work and testing the algorithms I had developed.

Continuing my PhD journey with Hahn-Schikard after our lab's relocation to Freiburg in 2022, my focus shifted towards optimizing my deep learning models for deployment on wearable prototypes. This optimization includes model compression, demonstrating my practical orientation and dedication to making innovations ready for the real world. Furthermore, I am involved in developing AI-driven methods for the early detection of chronic digestive disorders, such as Inflammatory Bowel Disease. I am dedicated to push the boundaries of medical diagnostics and ultimately improving patient care.
