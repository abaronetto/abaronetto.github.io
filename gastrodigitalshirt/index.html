Introduction

Common digestive disorders are often chronic and have a negative impact on patients' quality of life. Among the diagnostic methods employed by gastroenterologists, auscultation stands out as a non-invasive approach. Auscultation involves listening to bodily sounds for a brief duration.

Our focus lies in exploring the significance of low-amplitude bowel sounds generated during peristaltic contractions as potential indicators of chronic gastrointestinal conditions. Bowel Sound (BS) events are infrequent, random, and possess considerably low amplitude in comparison to other bodily sounds. Due to the sporadic nature of BS events, conventional short-term clinical auscultation may capture very few or none of them. How meaningful biomarkers related to digestion could be, when derived with such limited insight into the digestive process?

Innovation


We designed the GastroDigitalShirt, an intelligent T-Shirt designed for continuous acoustic monitoring of the abdomen over extended periods. The wearable prototype incorporates eight compact microphones linked to a low-energy wearable device. Crafted from elastane fabric, the T-Shirt offers high stretchability and comfort. The GastroDigitalShirt comes in different sizes to fit the largest population possible.

With the GastroDigitalShirt we recorded BS across different digestion phases in healthy participants as well as patients with diagnosed Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD). Utilizing our prototype, we identified four distinct pattern clusters of BS, each of which corresponds to known BS types.


To ease expert analysis of the audio data gathered with the GastroDigitalShirt, we proposed a segment-based approach to retrieve from the continuous recordings audio sections with a high probability of containing BS events. We applied pretrained Deep Neural Network architectures to segment-based BS spotting and showed that transfer learning can drastically reduce the amount of audio data to be manually reviewed by auscultation experts.

In future work, we plan to investigate novel digestion-related biomarkers from BS patterns as well as to develop automated BS segmentation methods.

Awards and Press

The GastroDigitalShirt received in 2019 the DGVS Innovation Award “Digital Gastroenterology” by the German Society for Gastroenterology, Digestive and Metabolic Diseases. The award recognizes projects to promote the development of digital innovations in gastroenterology.
